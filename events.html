22June

Sangeet

  • 6:30 onwards
  • Ashirwad Palace

The term “Sangeet” is a Sanskrit word that when translated into English it means “as sung together.” Sangeet Ceremony normally takes place 2-3 days prior to the wedding ceremony. This ceremony has both women and men where everyone celebrates, sings, dance and enjoys. This is the perfect time for the wedding guest to meet one another and get to know each other. Both the bridal and groom’s party will learn songs and perform at the sangeet ceremony. There would be live singing (band) with traditional Indian instruments, like dhol, piano, drums, guitar, and more. The band will be performing Taali Garba, 3 Taali Garba, Dandiya Raas, Heench, Sanedo, Kaka Bapa na Poriya and much more.

24June

The Baraat

  • 8:30am - 9:30am
  • Hanover Marriott

  • The groom arrives at the wedding ceremony location with his baraat of family and friends. Many grooms choose to arrive on horseback or in a horse and carriage. The Baraat gathers together and everyone dances in celebration. Traditionally Gujaratis did not marry someone from their own town so the baraat signified the groom entering the brides village. The baraat can become a large procession, with its own band & dancers. The groom and his horse are covered in finery and do not usually take part in the dancing and singing; that is left to the "baraatis" or people accompanying the procession. The term baraati is also more generically used to describe any invitee from the groom's side.
